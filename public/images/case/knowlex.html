Knowlex

UX Research | UX Design | UI Design | Usability Testing | Key Takeaways

Overview

Knowledge is probably the greatest power of all. Affordable education has become possible over the years thanks to technology.

Role: UX Researcher, UX/UI Designer

Toolkit: Figma, Adobe CC, FigJam, Pencil and Paper

UX Research

Background

Knowlex is an end-to-end app where users can exchange knowledge in three simple steps:

  • Teach what you know
  • Earn credits
  • Purchase lessons you want to learn

Research Goals

Investigate how people relate to EdTech platforms and understand how users can swap different types of knowledge through a tech product.

Methodologies

  • Competitive Analysis
  • User Interviews

User Interviews

Five people were interviewed remotely via Google Meet, including students, teachers, and casual learners.

Personas

Nick Green

Age: 31 | Job: Administrative Consultant

Bio: Uses EdTech products extensively to improve soft skills and boost knowledge in Administrative Law.

Goals

  • Integrate learning into work and personal life
  • Access a supportive community

Frustrations

  • Struggles with time management
  • Feels overwhelmed by too many learning choices

Christina Mottershead

Age: 35 | Job: Visual Merchandiser Manager

Bio: Considering a career change but struggling with financial constraints and online learning skepticism.

Goals

  • Take a Project Management course
  • Earn certifications to enhance her CV

Frustrations

  • Fears procrastination
  • Worries about online learning effectiveness

UX Design

Project Goals

Align business objectives with user needs to maximize engagement and retention.

Features Roadmap

Prioritized essential features such as user signup, settings, search, and a flexible credits system.

Information Architecture

Built a sitemap with primary navigation focusing on teaching, learning, and managing credits.

User Flows

Designed key user flows including:

  • Become a teacher
  • Redeem credits for lessons

UI Design

Brand Identity

The logo features a sphinx, symbolizing intelligence and wisdom. The name "Knowlex" combines "Knowledge" and "Exchange".

UI Components

Color palette: 1 primary, 2 secondary, and 3 neutral colors.

Typography: Mulish, chosen for its readability and modern style.

Usability Testing & Iterations

Prototype Enhancements

  • Added an activation message for account creation
  • Implemented loading animations for smoother transitions
  • Refined checkout experience with better credit display

Key Takeaways

Challenges

  • Finding a unique angle in a competitive EdTech space
  • Prioritizing features effectively in the MVP

Lessons Learned

  • Simplicity in design improves user experience
  • Building a strong UI library speeds up the process
